✨ Lesson 7 · Education · AI · Hype

Why is there a ✨ sparkle ✨ on every button?

A college professor noticed: every single app he uses sprouted an AI feature this year. He has a theory about why — and it has very little to do with whether AI is actually any good.

Intro

You know that little sparkle icon that's appeared on every button of every app — the one offering to "help" you write your text, summarize your email, generate your homework? ✨ Hank Green noticed it everywhere. He's annoyed. And his theory connects the sparkles to a bigger story about how schools and companies decide what tech to use.

Before watching — open your phone. Scroll through the apps you used today. Count the ✨ sparkles. Write the number down.

Watch · about 6.5 minutes

Hank talks fast — that's fine, you can rewind. He gets through: sparkles everywhere → admins using AI to write emails → a VR system the dean bought for no reason → his theory about why this keeps happening.

Words worth knowing

Try to guess each meaning first, then tap to reveal.

  • doubtful, not convinced

  • the plan/outline of a course

  • a formal judgment of how someone is doing

  • the rule that you do your own work honestly

  • an official order to do something

  • someone who runs an organization — here: school officials, not teachers

  • when prices and hype collapse after being too high (like a real bubble popping)

Solo task · Audit your apps

List apps and websites you use. For each one, decide if the AI feature is useful, annoying, or invisible (you've never used it). Be honest.

App or site verdict

At the bottom of your list, answer in one sentence: who do you think is asking for all this AI?

Discuss with Oma and Opa

  1. Show them your audit. Are they surprised by where AI is showing up?
  2. Hank's point: schools and companies often adopt new tech to look up-to-date, not because it works. Have Oma and Opa seen this happen before? (Ask about: smartboards, iPads in schools, "the metaverse," "the cloud.")
  3. If your teacher started using AI to grade your work next year, would you tell your parents? Would you be upset? Why or why not?
  4. Hank is annoyed. But he's also using AI features (like everyone). Is he being a hypocrite, or is that just modern life?

Stretch

Hank mentions his college spent its IT budget on a VR system "because the dean mentioned VR." Find one other example of a school or company spending money on a tech trend that didn't work out. (Hint: Google "Google Glass," "Meta Quest in schools," "iPad classrooms.")
