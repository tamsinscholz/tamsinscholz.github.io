# Why is there a sparkle on every button?

**Time:** ~30 min
**Materials:** `videos/The truth is dead, and I blame Air Bud...mp4`, paper + pen
**Video:** Hank Green — *The truth is dead, and I blame Air Bud* (segment **00:00 – 06:30**)

---

## Hook
You know that little sparkle icon that's appeared on every button of every app in the last year — the one offering to "help" you write your text, summarize your email, generate your homework? A professor noticed it everywhere. He's annoyed. And he has a theory about why it's happening — and it has very little to do with whether AI is actually good.

## Warmup (solo, 3 min)
Open your phone. Scroll through the apps you used today. **Count the sparkle icons (✨).** Write the number down.

## Watch (~7 min)
Play **00:00 to 06:30**. Hank Green talks fast — that's fine, you can rewind.

He goes through:
- AI sparkles on every website he uses
- His college's administrators using AI to write emails
- A virtual reality system the dean bought in 2015 that nobody wanted
- His theory: institutions adopt new tech to *look* relevant, not because it works

## Vocabulary

| Word | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **skeptical** | doubtful, not convinced |
| **syllabus** | the plan/outline of a course |
| **evaluation** | a formal judgment of how someone is doing |
| **academic integrity** | the rule that you do your own work honestly |
| **mandate** | an official order to do something |
| **administrator** | someone who runs an organization (here: school officials, not teachers) |
| **bubble (bursting)** | when prices/hype collapse after being too high (like a real bubble popping) |

## Solo task (10 min)
Make a 2-column list:

| App or website I use | Does it have AI features now? Useful, annoying, or invisible? |
|---|---|

List at least 5 — Instagram, Google, Snapchat, Discord, your school's website, whatever. Be honest. If you've never used the AI feature, mark it *invisible*.

Then, at the bottom, answer in one sentence: **who do you think is asking for all this AI?**

## Discussion with Oma/Opa (10–15 min)

1. Show them your list. **Are they surprised** by where AI is showing up?
2. Hank's point: schools and companies often adopt new tech to look up-to-date, not because it works. Have Oma/Opa seen this happen before? (Ask them about: smartboards, iPads in schools, the "metaverse," "the cloud.")
3. If your teacher started using AI to grade your work next year, would you tell your parents? Would you be upset? Why or why not?
4. Hank is annoyed. But he's also using AI features (like everyone). Is he being a hypocrite, or is that just modern life?

## Stretch
Hank mentions his college spent its IT budget on a VR system "because the dean mentioned VR." Find one other example of a school or company spending money on a tech trend that didn't work out. (Hint: Google *"Google Glass," "Meta Quest in schools," "iPad classrooms"*.)
