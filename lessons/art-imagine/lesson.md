# Make something that didn't exist this morning

**Time:** ~30 min (the lightest lesson of the week — designed as a reset)
**Materials:** a device with internet, paper + pen or pencils, optional: camera/phone
**Site:** https://imaginemore.art

---

## Hook
You've spent the week reading, listening, watching, analyzing. Today is the opposite: today you make a thing. It doesn't have to be good. It just has to not have existed when you woke up.

## Explore (10 min, solo)
Open https://imaginemore.art and poke around. Don't plan. Click things. See what's there.

While exploring, keep a tiny notebook page open and jot down:
- one thing that felt **fun**
- one thing that felt **weird**
- one thing you'd want to **steal** (idea, style, color, prompt — anything)

## Make (15 min, solo)
Pick **one** of these:

- **Option A — Remix:** find something on the site you like and make a version of it that's yours. Different subject, different mood, different color.
- **Option B — Pair with a song:** make a piece that visually matches *Aura* or *Schrei nach Liebe* from your German lessons. (Tone is the connection, not literal subject.)
- **Option C — Self-portrait, not your face:** make something that represents *you* this summer — without showing your face. Could be a still life of the 5 things on your bedside table, a map of the route between your room and the kitchen, anything.

Don't overthink. 15 minutes, stop when the timer rings, hand it over even if it's "not done."

## Discussion with Oma/Opa (5–10 min)

1. Show them what you made. **Don't apologize for it first.** (No "it's not that good." Just show it.)
2. Explain: what choices did you make? What did you almost do, then change?
3. Ask Oma/Opa: **what would they have made, given the same 15 minutes?** Get them to describe it in words.
4. If you had another hour, what would you change or add?

## Stretch
Make a *second* tiny piece — but this time, give yourself a constraint:
- only 2 colors, OR
- only straight lines, OR
- only things you can find in the kitchen

Constraints are where ideas come from.

---

> **Why this lesson exists:** all the other lessons this week ask you to *consume and respond*. This one asks you to *generate*. Both muscles need exercise. Don't skip it just because it doesn't have vocab.
