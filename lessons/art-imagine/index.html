Lesson 5 · Art · Make

Make something that
didn't exist this morning.

All week you've been consuming. Reading, listening, watching. Today you make a thing. It doesn't have to be good. It just has to not have existed when you woke up.

Intro

This is your reset lesson. Open the site below, poke around for ten minutes without planning, then make one thing in fifteen. There's a timer on this page that won't let you overthink it.

Explore · about 10 minutes

Click around. Don't plan. See what's there.

imaginemore.art ↗

While you explore, jot down three tiny notes:

  • One thing that felt fun.
  • One thing that felt weird.
  • One thing you'd want to steal (idea, color, mood, style — anything).

Now make · 15 minutes

Pick one:

A

Remix

Find something on the site you like. Make a version of it that's yours — different subject, mood, or colour.

B

Pair with a song

Make a piece that visually matches Aura or Schrei nach Liebe. Tone is the connection — not literal subject.

C

Self-portrait, not your face

Make something that represents you this summer without showing your face. The 5 things on your bedside table. A map of your route to the kitchen. Anything.

When you're ready:

15:00

Stop when it rings. Hand it over even if it's "not done."

Discuss with Oma and Opa

  1. Show them what you made. Don't apologize for it first. No "it's not that good." Just show it.
  2. Explain: what choices did you make? What did you almost do, then change?
  3. Ask Oma and Opa: what would they have made, given the same 15 minutes? Get them to describe it in words.
  4. If you had another hour, what would you change or add?

Stretch

Make a second tiny piece — but this time, give yourself a constraint:

  • only 2 colors
  • only straight lines
  • only things you can find in the kitchen

Constraints are where ideas come from.
