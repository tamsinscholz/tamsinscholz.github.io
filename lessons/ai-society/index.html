Lesson 4 · Society · AI · Edition of 2026

Is AI the problem, or
just exposing one?

An argument: students write essays with AI; teachers grade them with AI. Nobody is sure who, if anyone, is actually learning anything. But maybe — just maybe — the problem isn't AI at all.

Intro

Everyone is panicking about AI in schools. The video below argues this isn't really an AI problem — it's showing us that school was already broken in a particular way. Was it?

Before watching, finish this sentence three different ways: "If everyone at my school suddenly stopped using AI tomorrow, the thing that would actually change is…"

Watch · about 8 minutes

The host is fast and sarcastic — that's the style. The first part introduces the topic; the second zooms in on education. Key claim to listen for: "Is it really an AI crisis, or is AI just revealing an already messed-up education system?"

Words worth knowing

Try to guess each meaning first, then tap to reveal.

  • to reveal something that was hidden

  • something you take for granted without proving it

  • doing the honest thing even when no one's watching

  • a moment when something serious is breaking

  • a test or evaluation of what someone knows

  • reacting in fear without a clear plan

  • causing more harm than good overall

Solo task · Three sentences

The video's argument has a shape: "X looks like a new problem caused by AI, but actually it's an old problem AI is just making visible." Try that shape on something from your own school.

saved · stays here for next time

Discuss with Oma and Opa

  1. Read your three sentences out loud. Do Oma and Opa agree with your "real question"?
  2. The video says students cheat with AI because the system asks them to do work that doesn't feel meaningful. Is that fair, or is it letting students off the hook?
  3. When Oma and Opa were in school, what was the "cheating" technology of their day? (Pocket calculators? Wikipedia? Photocopies of someone's notes?) How did teachers react then?
  4. If you were a principal, what's the one rule you'd make about AI in your school next September?

Stretch

The video frames AI as a "wake-up call." But wake-up calls only work if someone wakes up. Who would have to wake up here — students, teachers, parents, governments? Pick one and write what they'd have to actually do differently.
