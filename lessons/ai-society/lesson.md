# Is AI the problem, or is it exposing one?

**Time:** ~30 min
**Materials:** `videos/It's Not Really an AI Problem, Is It？.mp4`, paper + pen
**Video:** *It's Not Really an AI Problem, Is It?* (segment **00:00 – 08:30**)

---

## Hook
Everyone is panicking about AI in schools. Students write essays with AI. Teachers grade them with AI. Nobody is sure who, if anyone, is actually learning anything. But here's the twist: the video argues this isn't really an AI problem — it's showing us that school was already broken in a particular way. Was it?

## Warmup (solo, 3 min)
Finish this sentence three different ways:

> "If everyone at my school suddenly stopped using AI tomorrow, the thing that would actually change is…"

## Watch (~8 min)
Play **00:00 to ~08:30**. The host moves fast and is sarcastic — that's the style. The first part introduces the topic; the second part zooms in on **education** specifically.

Key claim to listen for: *"Is it really an AI crisis, or is AI just revealing an already messed-up education system?"*

## Vocabulary

| Word | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **expose** (a problem) | to reveal something that was hidden |
| **assumption** | something you take for granted without proving it |
| **integrity** | doing the honest thing even when no one's watching |
| **crisis** | a moment when something serious is breaking |
| **assessment** | a test or evaluation of what someone knows |
| **panicking** | reacting in fear without a clear plan |
| **net negative** | causing more harm than good overall |

## Solo task (10 min)
The video's argument has a shape: *"X looks like a new problem caused by AI, but actually it's an old problem AI is just making visible."*

Pick **one thing about school** that frustrates you (homework, grading, group projects, memorization for tests, whatever). Write 3 sentences:
1. **The thing:** what it is.
2. **The fake explanation:** how someone might blame AI for making it worse.
3. **The real question:** what's actually wrong with it, AI aside.

## Discussion with Oma/Opa (10–15 min)

1. **Read your 3 sentences out loud.** Do Oma/Opa agree with your "real question"?
2. The video says students cheat with AI because the *system* asks them to do work that doesn't feel meaningful. Is that fair, or is it letting students off the hook?
3. When Oma/Opa were in school, what was the "cheating" technology of their day? (Pocket calculators? Wikipedia? Photocopies of someone's notes?) How did teachers react then?
4. If you were a principal, what's the *one rule* you'd make about AI in your school next September?

## Stretch
The video frames AI as a "wake-up call." But wake-up calls only work if someone wakes up. Who would have to wake up here — students, teachers, parents, governments? Pick one and write what they'd have to actually *do* differently.
