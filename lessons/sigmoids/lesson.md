# Why nothing grows forever

**Time:** ~30 min
**Materials:** graph paper (or blank paper + ruler), pencil, eraser
**Reading:** Scott Alexander — *The Sigmoids Won't Save You* — https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/the-sigmoids-wont-save-you

---

## Hook
Things that grow fast look unstoppable — TikTok followers, a viral band, your height between 11 and 13. But every fast-growing thing in the real world eventually slows down. The shape this makes on a graph is called a **sigmoid** (an S-curve). Once you can see it, you spot it everywhere.

## Three shapes to know first (5 min, solo)

Draw these three curves on the same piece of paper. **Don't read the article yet.** Just draw what each name suggests:

1. **Linear** — straight line going up (you add 1 each year)
2. **Exponential** — curve that gets steeper and steeper (you double each year)
3. **Sigmoid** — slow start, fast middle, slow finish (an S, lying on its back)

Label which is which.

## Read (8–10 min)
Read Scott Alexander's article. It's short. Mark **2 sentences** that confused you and **1 sentence** that surprised you.

> Tip: the word *sigmoid* comes from the Greek letter sigma (σ), which looks like an S.

## Vocabulary

| Word | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **exponential** | growing by multiplying (×2, ×2, ×2…) instead of adding |
| **plateau** | a flat top — growth has stopped |
| **inflection point** | the spot on an S-curve where growth flips from speeding up to slowing down |
| **saturation** | when something is "full" and can't grow more (the market is saturated, the sponge is saturated) |
| **trajectory** | the path something is taking over time |

## Solo task (10 min) — Sketch four sigmoids from your own life

Draw 4 quick graphs. **X-axis = time, Y-axis = how much.** Mark the inflection point and the plateau on each. Don't worry about exact numbers — just the *shape*.

1. **Your height** from age 0 to age 30. *(Where's the plateau?)*
2. **Number of followers** on a TikTok that goes viral and then fades.
3. **How well you play piano** if you practice 1 hour every day starting today, for 10 years.
4. **Number of people who own a smartphone in Canada**, from 2007 (iPhone launch) to now.

Question to answer underneath: which one was **hardest** to draw, and why?

## Discussion with Oma/Opa (10 min)

1. Show them your 4 graphs. Do they agree on where each one plateaus?
2. Ask: **"Name one thing you saw take off like a rocket in your lifetime and then plateau."** (TV? Cars? CDs? Air travel? Email?)
3. People keep saying "AI is growing exponentially." If sigmoids are the real shape — when do they think it might bend? What would have to be true for it to bend?
4. Scott's point: predicting **when** the curve bends is the hard part. Why is that so hard?
