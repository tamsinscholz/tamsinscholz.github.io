Lesson 3 · Math · Growth curves

Why nothing grows
forever.

Things that grow fast look unstoppable — until they don't. The shape this makes on a graph is called a sigmoid. Once you can see it, you spot it everywhere.

Intro

TikTok followers, a viral band, your height between 11 and 13 — they all look like they could keep climbing. But every fast-growing thing in the real world eventually slows down. That slowdown is the S-curve. The name comes from the Greek letter σ ("sigma"), which looks like an S.

Words worth knowing

Try to guess each meaning first, then tap to reveal.

  • growing by multiplying (×2, ×2, ×2…) instead of adding

  • a flat top — growth has stopped

  • the spot on an S-curve where growth flips from speeding up to slowing down

  • when something is "full" and can't grow more (the market is saturated; the sponge is saturated)

  • the path something is taking over time

Solo task · Sketch four sigmoids from your own life

Use real paper and pencil. Draw 4 quick graphs. X-axis = time. Y-axis = how much. Don't worry about exact numbers — just the shape.

  1. Your height from age 0 to age 30. (Where's the plateau?)
  2. Number of followers on a TikTok that goes viral and then fades.
  3. How well you play piano if you practice 1 hour every day for 10 years.
  4. Number of people in Canada who own a smartphone, from 2007 (iPhone launch) to now.

Underneath, answer in one sentence: which was hardest to draw, and why?

Discuss with Oma and Opa

  1. Show them your 4 graphs. Do they agree on where each one plateaus?
  2. Ask: "Name one thing you saw take off like a rocket in your lifetime and then plateau." (TV? Cars? CDs? Air travel? Email?)
  3. People keep saying "AI is growing exponentially." If sigmoids are the real shape — when do they think it might bend? What would have to be true for it to bend?
  4. Scott's point: predicting when the curve bends is the hard part. Why is that so hard?
