Lesson 2 · German · Slang

Was ist Aura?

Slang you don't learn in textbooks. What is a German teenager actually saying when she sings "Meine Handtasche ist Aura"?

Intro

Every generation invents words their parents don't understand. Aura is one of them right now — and it doesn't mean what it meant 20 years ago. The song below is the unofficial anthem of how teenagers in 2025 use the word.

Before listening — write down what you think "aura" means in English. Two sentences.

Listen · about 1:30 minutes

Don't read the lyrics yet — just listen. Try to catch the chorus. Then play it again with this question: what is she bragging about, and what is she NOT bragging about?

Words worth knowing

Try to guess each meaning first, then tap a chip to reveal.

Solo task · Write the caption

Write a 4-line caption in German for an imaginary Instagram post about your day at the grandparents'. Use at least 3 of the vocab words above. Bonus: use one of them in a way that's a bit ironic.

Discuss with Oma and Opa

  1. Listen to the song together. Did they get any of it? Which words tripped them up?
  2. Did Oma/Opa have a word like Aura or krass when they were 13? What did it mean?
  3. The singer says "Mich juckt das nicht" about her crush's ex. Is that confidence, or is she protesting too much? How would Oma read it?
  4. In English, would you ever say "I have an aura"? Why does it work in German but feel weird in English?

Stretch

The whole song is built on bragging — but it's also self-aware and funny. Find one line that feels like a joke at the singer's own expense. Why is it funny?
