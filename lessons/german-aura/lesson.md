# Aura — modern German you don't get in class

**Time:** ~30 min
**Materials:** `videos/Leni Woess - Aura ...mp4`, paper + pen
**Song:** Leni Woess feat. Toby Woess — *Aura* (2024)

---

## Hook
Every generation invents words their parents don't understand. "Aura" is one of them right now — and it doesn't mean what it meant 20 years ago. What is a German teenager actually saying when she sings *"Mein Handtasche ist Aura"*?

## Warmup (solo, 3 min)
Before listening — write down what *you* think "aura" means in English. Two sentences.

## Listen (5 min)
Play the song from **00:00 to 01:30**. Don't read lyrics yet — just listen. Try to catch the chorus.

Then listen *again* with this question in mind: **what is the singer bragging about, and what is she NOT bragging about?**

## Vocabulary — 10 words & phrases worth knowing

These aren't textbook words. They're the slang and idiomatic stuff that makes you sound like a real teenager, not a tourist.

| German | Meaning | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Aura haben** | "to have aura" | Gen Z slang — vibe, presence, *it factor* |
| **die Karre** | car (slang) | literally "cart" — never say "Auto" in this song |
| **der/die Lauch** | loser / wimp | literally "leek" (the vegetable!) |
| **flexen** | to show off | from English "to flex" |
| **der Hater** | hater | German keeps English word + adds article |
| **das Lowy** | Louis Vuitton | brand abbreviation, very Gen Z |
| **die Ex** | ex-girlfriend | short and sharp — same as English |
| **mich juckt das nicht** | "it doesn't itch me" = I don't care | idiomatic — very common |
| **krass** | wild / intense / cool | the Swiss-army knife of German slang |
| **die Charisma** | charisma | same word, German pronunciation: ka-RIS-ma |

## Solo task (10 min)
Write a 4-line caption (in German!) for an imaginary Instagram post about your day at the grandparents'. Use at least **3 of the vocab words above**, and at least one in a way that's a bit ironic.

Example tone: *"Meine Karre ist ein Fahrrad mit Korb. Aura? Definitely."*

## Discussion with Oma/Opa (10–15 min)

1. Listen to the song together. **Did they get any of it?** Which words tripped them up?
2. Did Oma/Opa have a word like *Aura* or *krass* when they were 13? What did it mean?
3. The singer says *"Mich juckt das nicht"* about her crush's ex. Is that confidence, or is she protesting too much? How would Oma read it?
4. In English, would you ever say "I have an aura"? Why does it work in German but feel weird in English?

## Stretch
The whole song is built on bragging — but it's also kind of self-aware and funny. Find one line that feels like a joke at the singer's own expense. Why is it funny?
