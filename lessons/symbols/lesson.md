# The # is actually two letters. Guess which?

**Time:** ~30 min
**Materials:** `videos/The hidden logic behind #, @, & and §.mp4`, paper + pen, a phone or camera (for the scavenger hunt)
**Video:** RobWords — *The hidden logic behind #, @, & and §* (segment **00:00 – ~06:30**, just the `#` story)

---

## Hook
The symbol you call "hashtag" — the one that runs your whole social media life — was once two Roman letters scratched onto a clay tablet to mean "12 ounces of grapes." The path from there to TikTok is one of the more ridiculous stories in writing. Let's trace it.

## Warmup (solo, 2 min)
Before the video — what do you call the `#` symbol? Write down every name you can think of. (There are at least five.)

## Watch (~6 min)
Play **00:00 to ~06:30**. The host (RobWords) walks through the full origin story of `#`:

- Romans weighing grapes by the *libra* (pound)
- Abbreviated *lb*, written with a line through it (℔)
- Scribes writing fast, making it loopy — turning into `#`
- How it became the "pound sign" on phones, then "hash," then "hashtag" on Twitter
- Why mathematicians call it the *octothorpe*

Stop when he moves on to the next symbol (around 6:30).

## Vocabulary

| Word | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **abbreviation** | a shortened way of writing a word |
| **ligature** | two letters joined into one shape (æ, &) |
| **scribe** | a person whose job was writing things by hand, before printing |
| **ornate** | very decorated, fancy |
| **etymology** | the history of where a word or symbol came from |

## Solo task — Symbol Scavenger Hunt (10 min)

Walk around Oma/Opa's house. Find **as many of these symbols as you can** in books, on keyboards, on packaging, on signs, on the TV remote:

- `#` (hash / pound / octothorpe)
- `@` (at)
- `&` (ampersand)
- `§` (section sign — common in German books!)
- `%` (percent)
- `©` (copyright)
- `$` or `£` or `€` (currency)
- One **bonus symbol** of your choice

Photograph or sketch each one. Note **where you found it** and **what it meant in context**.

## Discussion with Oma/Opa (10 min)

1. Show them your scavenger hunt photos/sketches. Did they know any of the histories?
2. **Pick one symbol you found and ask them: "What do you call this?"** Compare to what you call it. Any differences?
3. The hashtag became famous because Twitter borrowed an old telephone-keypad symbol. **Can they think of another symbol that got a totally new meaning recently?** (Hint: 🍑, 💀, the "thumbs up" — emojis count!)
4. The `§` symbol shows up *constantly* in German legal and academic writing — and almost never in English. Why might that be?

## Stretch
Pick one symbol from your scavenger hunt that the video *didn't* cover (e.g., `%`, `©`, `$`). Guess its origin first, then look it up. Were you close?

Suggested rabbit hole: **`&`** is also a ligature of two Latin letters — which ones? (Hint: it used to be the 27th letter of the alphabet.)
