Lesson 1 · Linguistics

The # is actually two letters.

Guess which? Then watch a short video that traces the strangest history on your keyboard.

Intro

You call it a hashtag. Someone older calls it the pound sign. A mathematician calls it the octothorpe. They're all right — and the symbol started as two Latin letters scratched onto a tablet to mean "twelve ounces of grapes."

Before the video — write down every name you can think of for #. There are at least five.

Discuss with Oma and Opa

  1. Show them your scavenger hunt. Did they know any of the histories?
  2. Pick one symbol — ask them: "What do you call this?" Compare to your name for it. Any differences?
  3. # became famous because Twitter borrowed it from old telephone keypads. Can they think of another symbol that got a totally new meaning recently? (hint: emojis count.)
  4. The § symbol shows up everywhere in German legal and academic writing — but almost never in English. Why might that be?

Stretch

Pick one symbol the video didn't cover — %, ©, or $. Guess its origin first. Then look it up. Were you close?

Rabbit hole: the & symbol is a ligature of two Latin letters. Which two? (it used to be the 27th letter of the alphabet.)
